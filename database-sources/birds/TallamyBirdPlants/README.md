# Tallamy - Most Valuable Plant Genera for Bird/Wildlife Biodiversity

**Source:** Doug Tallamy, University of Delaware (2018)
**URL:** https://canr.udel.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/16/2018/10/30121131/20-Most-Valuable-for-Biodiversity.pdf
**Data:** 42 plant genera ranked by Lepidoptera (caterpillar) species support
**Region:** Mid-Atlantic (genus-level data applicable nationwide)

## About

Doug Tallamy's research demonstrates that **96% of terrestrial bird species feed caterpillars to their nestlings**. The number of Lepidoptera (butterfly/moth) species whose caterpillars feed on a plant genus directly predicts that plant's value for supporting bird populations.

This dataset ranks the top 42 plant genera — 21 woody plants and 21 perennials — by the number of caterpillar species they support. Oak (*Quercus*) leads with 557 Lepidoptera species, making it the single most important genus for bird conservation.

## Why This Matters for Fire-Wise Landscaping

When selecting fire-resistant native plants for landscaping, choosing genera that also support high Lepidoptera diversity creates landscapes that are both **fire-safe AND bird-habitat rich**. Cross-referencing this data with our fire resistance datasets helps identify the best dual-purpose plants.
