# S.A.F.E. LANDSCAPES - Southern California Guidebook

**Source:** University of California Cooperative Extension
**Authors:** Drill, S.L., Quarles, S.L., Borel, V.T., Ready, D., Casanova, J.
**Year:** 2009
**Pages:** 45
**URL:** https://ucanr.edu/sites/safelandscapes/files/93415.pdf

## About

S.A.F.E. (Sustainable And Fire SafE) Landscapes provides guidelines for creating and maintaining fire-safe, environmentally friendly landscapes in the wildland-urban interface of Southern California.

## Data Content

**This guidebook does NOT contain structured plant tables or databases.** It is an educational guide covering:
- Fire behavior and climate in Southern California
- Defensible space and fire-safe landscape design
- Seasonal maintenance calendar (spring, summer, fall, winter)
- Problem plants to avoid (inline mentions: Castor Bean, Artichoke Thistle, Brooms, Pampas Grass, Pines, Junipers, Cypress, etc.)
- Fire-resistant plant characteristics
- Invasive plant awareness
- Erosion and post-fire recovery
- Reference websites and books

## Value for Project

While not a data source for extraction, this document provides:
- Authoritative definitions of fire-resistant plant characteristics
- Problem plant examples with photos and descriptions
- Southern California fire ecology context
- Seasonal maintenance guidelines relevant to fire safety
