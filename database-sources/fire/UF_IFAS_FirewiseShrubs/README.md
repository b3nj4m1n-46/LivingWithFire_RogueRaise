# UF/IFAS - Selecting Firewise Shrubs to Reduce Wildfire Risk

**Source:** University of Florida IFAS Extension, Publication FOR272
**Authors:** Hermansen-Báez, A., Crandall, R.M., Zipperer, W.C., Long, A.J., Behm, A.L., McKinstry, D., and Andreu, A.
**URL:** https://edis.ifas.ufl.edu/publication/FR147

## About

This is a companion fact sheet to the NIST/USDA flammability study of 34 ornamental shrubs. The 3-page publication summarizes the study findings and provides firewise landscaping guidance for homeowners in the wildland-urban interface.

## Relationship to Other Datasets

**The plant data from this study is already captured in `NIST_USDA_Flammability/`** — same 34 shrubs with flammability ratings (22 low, 8 moderate, 4 high). This folder contains only the source PDF as a supplemental reference.

## Key Findings (from source)

- 22 of 34 shrubs have **low flammability** — appropriate for firewise planting near homes
- 8 shrubs are **moderately flammable** — should be cautiously planted in defensible space
- 4 shrubs are **highly flammable** — not recommended near homes
- Even low-flammability plants may be more flammable during droughts
